STRC: the obligation that grows when you need it to shrink

STRC is a roughly $5.0 billion perpetual preferred paying 11.5% a year, about $48 million a month. It sits inside the senior stack, has no claim on the Bitcoin, and never matures. To keep it trading near its $100 par, Strategy resets the rate every month, and so far it has only gone up: 9% at launch in July 2025 to 11.5% by March 2026.

In a crash that becomes a trap. Defending par means raising the rate, which raises the cash drain, which forces more Bitcoin sales, exactly when the at-the-market machine that funds those dividends is breaking. Missed dividends do not disappear either; they accumulate and compound at the prevailing rate. Turn on "defend par" in the controls and push the STRC rate up to watch it feed the claim ratio and pull the final stack down.

Educational model, not investment advice. Starting figures reflect Strategy's reported holdings and a public stress test by Adam Livingston (@AdamBLiv). Bitcoin and MSTR prices are point-in-time and editable. The model assumes no new buys, no new equity issuance, and obligations serviced first from cash and then from BTC sales.
